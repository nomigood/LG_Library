재단소개

LG Yonam Foundation carries out educational and cultural public services.

Activity

LG Yonam Foundation, to which LG Sangnam Library belongs, was established in December 1969, by In-Hwoi Koo, the founder of LG Group. He acknowledged the demand for leaders in science and technology, and aimed to train young generation to become future leaders of Korea. LG Yonam Foundation started realizing its vision with supporting Jinju Municipal YonamLibrary, and have been serving the public through nurturing education and culture since then. Its domainincludes providing scholarship, supporting educational institutions, supporting professors studying overseas, and managing LG Sangnam Library and LG Arts Center.
LG Yonam Foundation is operating and managing LG Arts Center and LG Sangnam Library. It also supports the Harvard Center for Korean Studies, supports JinjuYonam Library, and carries out several other cultural and educational public services.

Scholarship Programs

LG Yonam Foundation has been offering scholarship to undergraduate students for more than 30 years. Since 2001, our beneficiaries are chosen among graduate students. And since 1989, for the first time as a private enterprise foundation, LG Yonam Foundation is offering financial support for university professors studying and researching overseas.

LG Art Center

LG Yonam Foundation built LG Arts Center, after five years of construction at a total cost of 65 billion Won, LG Arts Center, a superb cultural venue with the capacity of 1,103 seats. LG Arts center manifests LGs philosophy of corporate contribution to society through providing cultural infrastructure. It received USITT Architecture Merit Award in 2001, and also received the 4thMecenat Award Grand Prize in 2003, for excellent building and management. As a multi-purpose auditorium that can accommodate various fields of art, from music to dance to drama to choral, LG Arts Center is providing extended chance to experience superior performing arts of Korea and everywhere else. LG Arts Center also won the PAMAK Performing Arts Management Award 3 times in a row, from 2010 to 2012.
